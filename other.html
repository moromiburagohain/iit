Other Activities

1. FDP Structure and Content

The programs are designed to align with the National Education Policy (NEP 2020) and focus on crucial, high-demand technical areas:

Program Type Duration / Credit Target Audience Emerging Topics Covered
Faculty Development Program (FDP) ~40 Hours (4 Credits) Faculty in HEIs AI/ML, Data Science, Cyber Security, Chip Design, 5G/6G Wireless, Drones (UAS/RPAS), AR/VR, and Medical Electronics
Refresher Course ~80–90 Hours (8 Credits) Faculty in HEIs Deeper, more extensive training in core or emerging areas
Joint FDP / External Course Varies Professionals, Students, Faculty Collaboratively offered programs such as Full Stack Software Engineering with AI (with partners like AlmaBetter)

2. Strategic Role of Joint FDPs (Phase II)

The Joint FDPs and the overall Hub-and-Spoke model are essential to the Phase II strategy for achieving a nationwide impact:

Key Area Description
Spoke Centre Handholding The primary goal is to mentor and establish Spoke Centres at Tier 2/Tier 3 institutions (like the proposed one at JSS). The Hub Academies (IITG) run initial FDPs at these Spokes to handhold local faculty and build their capacity to conduct training independently.
Wider Beneficiary Base While FDPs primarily target faculty, Joint/External courses are designed for students, industry professionals, and government officials (often on a chargeable basis) to ensure the Academy’s sustainability, as outlined in the Project Completion Report format.
